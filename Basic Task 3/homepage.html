About Coffee

Coffee is one of the world’s most popular beverages, originating in Ethiopia and evolving into a global cultural icon. Made from seeds of the Coffea plant, it undergoes a complex process of cultivation, roasting, and brewing to achieve its rich flavors. Coffee drives economies in major producing countries like Brazil and Colombia and serves as a social and cultural connector worldwide.

History of Coffee

Coffee’s history dates back to 9th-century Ethiopia, where it was first discovered by a goat herder. It spread to Yemen, and by the 15th century, coffee houses flourished in the Middle East. By the 17th century, coffee reached Europe, sparking the rise of coffee houses known as "penny universities".

Types of Coffee

Explore the different types of coffee enjoyed around the world:

  • Espresso: A strong, concentrated coffee brewed by forcing hot water through finely-ground coffee beans.
  • Latte: Made with a shot of espresso and steamed milk, topped with a thin layer of foam.
  • Cappuccino: Similar to a latte but with more foam and often topped with cocoa powder or cinnamon.
  • Americano: A shot of espresso diluted with hot water, giving it a similar strength to drip coffee.
  • Macchiato: Espresso with a small amount of milk or foam, creating a strong yet creamy drink.
  • Mocha: A combination of espresso, steamed milk, and chocolate, often topped with whipped cream.
